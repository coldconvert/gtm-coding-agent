# Objection Handling

## Price / Budget
**"It's too expensive."**
→ [Response framework: reframe cost vs. cost of inaction]

**"We don't have budget right now."**
→ [Response: timeline, trigger events, stay in touch strategy]

## Timing
**"Not the right time."**
→ [Response: what would make it the right time?]

**"We're locked into [competitor] for another year."**
→ [Response: pilot alongside, migration plan]

## Authority
**"I need to check with my boss / team."**
→ [Response: offer to join the conversation, provide internal champion materials]

**"IT/Security needs to approve."**
→ [Response: security docs, SOC2, architecture diagrams]

## Need
**"We're fine with our current process."**
→ [Response: quantify the hidden cost of status quo]

**"We tried something similar before and it didn't work."**
→ [Response: what was different? What would need to be true this time?]

## Competition
**"We're already using [Competitor]."**
→ [Response: specific differentiation, migration ease, what they're missing]

**"Can you do everything [Competitor] does?"**
→ [Response: focus on what matters for their use case, not feature parity]
