# Platform Playbook

Each platform has different rules for reach, engagement, and voice adaptation. Same message, different delivery.

---

## LinkedIn

### Format Rules
- **Hook:** First line must earn the "see more" click. Lead with a surprising claim, number, or contrast.
- **Paragraphs:** 1-3 sentences max. White space is your friend.
- **Length:** 800-1300 characters for regular posts. Longer for deep dives (with formatting).
- **Links:** NEVER put links in the post body. LinkedIn throttles reach. Put links in the first comment.
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 relevant hashtags at the bottom. Don't overdo it.
- **Images:** Posts with images get more reach. Carousels (PDF uploads) get the most.
- **Engagement:** Reply to every comment in the first 2 hours. The algorithm rewards conversation.

### Voice Adaptation
- More personal than blog. Use "I" and tell stories.
- Professional but not corporate. You're talking to peers, not presenting to a board.
- End with a thought or question that invites comment (but not "agree?" — that's engagement bait).

### What Works
- Contrarian takes with evidence
- Behind-the-scenes of how you do things
- Specific numbers and results
- "Here's what I learned" format
- Short case studies

### What Doesn't Work
- Links in body
- Engagement bait ("like if you agree")
- Generic advice without specifics
- Overly promotional posts
- Walls of text

## Email (Outbound)

### Format Rules
- **Subject line:** 3-6 words. Lowercase. No clickbait.
- **Body:** 50-100 words max. Phone-screen friendly.
- **Paragraphs:** 2-3 sentences max.
- **CTA:** One ask. Clear. Low commitment. ("Worth a 15-min call?" not "Schedule a demo to see how we can transform your revenue operations.")
- **Signature:** Name + title + one link. No banners, quotes, or social icons.

### Voice Adaptation
- Most direct version of your voice. No warm-up paragraphs.
- Write like you're emailing a busy colleague, not a stranger.
- Personalization must be real (reference something specific about their company/role), not templated ("I noticed your company is growing...").

### Sequence Structure
1. **Email 1 (Day 1):** Observation + relevance. "I noticed [specific thing]. We help companies like yours [specific outcome]."
2. **Email 2 (Day 3):** Value-add. Share something useful. No ask.
3. **Email 3 (Day 7):** Social proof. "[Similar company] saw [result]. Would this be relevant?"
4. **Email 4 (Day 14):** Direct ask. "Is this worth 15 minutes?"
5. **Email 5 (Day 21):** Break-up. "Seems like timing isn't right. I'll check back in Q3."

## Blog

### Format Rules
- **Length:** 800-2000 words. Depends on topic depth.
- **Structure:** H1 title, intro paragraph, H2 sections, conclusion with takeaway.
- **Paragraphs:** 3-5 sentences. More detail than LinkedIn.
- **Code blocks:** Use them when showing technical content. Readers scan for code.
- **Images:** One hero image + screenshots/diagrams where helpful.
- **SEO:** Target one keyword. Use it in H1, first paragraph, one H2, and meta description.

### Voice Adaptation
- More thorough than LinkedIn. You have room to explain.
- Still conversational. Not academic.
- Show your work — include examples, code, screenshots.

---

## Twitter / X

### Format Rules
- **Single tweet:** 280 characters. Lead with the sharpest version of your point.
- **Thread:** First tweet is the hook. One idea per tweet. Number them (1/, 2/). Last tweet = summary + CTA.
- **No hashtags** in the thread body. Optional at the end.
- **Quote tweets** over replies for commentary.

### Voice Adaptation
- Sharpest, most compressed version of your voice.
- Hot takes are fine if you can back them up.
- Personality matters more here than any other platform.
