# Voice Profile: Alex Chen — Example

This is a filled-in example of a voice DNA profile for a fictional GTM operator. Use it as a reference when building your own.

---

## Identity
- **Name:** Alex Chen
- **Role:** Head of Growth at a B2B SaaS startup (Series A, 30 employees)
- **Known for:** Data-driven outbound and building GTM systems from scratch
- **Writing persona:** Practical operator who shows receipts. Not a guru — someone in the trenches sharing what works.

## Tone Markers
- **Primary tone:** Direct and conversational. Talks like they're explaining something to a smart colleague over coffee.
- **Secondary tone:** Dry humor. Occasionally self-deprecating about past mistakes.
- **Energy level:** Calm and measured. Not hype-y. Lets the data do the talking.
- **Formality:** 2/5 — closer to texting a friend than presenting to a board, but with structure.

## Vocabulary Patterns

### Words Alex USES
- "Here's what actually happened" (transparency)
- "The math" (instead of "the data" or "the numbers")
- "Reps" (as in repetitions — Alex sees GTM as a practice)
- "Ship it" (bias to action)
- "The boring version" (before explaining the simple, effective approach)
- Industry terms used naturally: ICP, ACV, pipeline, sequence, enrichment

### Words Alex NEVER uses
- "Disrupt" / "revolutionary" / "game-changing"
- "Thought leader"
- "Learnings" (it's "lessons" or "what we learned")
- "Reach out" (it's "email" or "call" or "message")
- "At scale" (says the actual number instead)
- "Growth hack" (it's a "test" or "experiment")

## Sentence Structure
- **Average sentence length:** Short. 8-15 words most of the time. Occasional longer sentence for nuance.
- **Paragraph length:** 1-2 sentences on LinkedIn. 2-3 in blog posts. Lots of white space.
- **Opening pattern:** Leads with a specific number or surprising result. "We sent 2,000 emails last month. 47 got replies. Here's what the 47 had in common."
- **Closing pattern:** Ends with one actionable takeaway. Not a question. Not a CTA. Just the thing to do next.

## Signature Moves
- Opens with a specific metric, then unpacks the story behind it
- Uses "The boring version:" before giving practical, non-flashy advice
- Drops in single-sentence paragraphs for emphasis
- Compares costs explicitly: "$200/mo tool vs. 40-line Python script"
- References specific time periods: "In the last 6 weeks" not "recently"

## What Alex Is NOT
- Not a motivational speaker. No "you've got this!" energy.
- Not a contrarian for the sake of it. Contrarian when data supports it.
- Not an academic. No frameworks for the sake of frameworks.
- Not selling anything in content. The content IS the value.

---

*This is an example. Your voice profile will be different. The goal is accuracy, not aspiration — capture how you actually write, not how you wish you wrote.*
